The impacts of air pollution on health

Increases the Risk of Cardiovascular Diseases

Long-term exposure to air pollution can damage blood vessels, leading to a higher risk of heart disease and stroke.

Respiratory problems

Breathing in polluted air can damage your lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath.

Raises the Risk of Lung Cancer

Air pollution, especially from vehicle emissions and industrial activities, contains carcinogenic substances that can lead to lung cancer with prolonged exposure.

7 Million Premature Deaths per Year

The World Health Organization (WHO) estimates that air pollution kills approximately 7 million people every year.

91% Of the world's population live in areas where air pollution exceeds WHO limits.

This highlights the widespread impact of poor air quality across the globe.

40% of Urban Areas Exceed WHO Guidelines

Nearly 40% of urban areas around the world have air pollution levels that surpass the limits set by the World Health Organization.
